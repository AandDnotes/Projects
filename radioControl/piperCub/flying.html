Radio Controlled Aircraft

- Flying

First Flight

The day finally arrived. I am definitely a fine weather flyer. Even when it is quite warm at home, up on the South Downs it can be quite chilly and certainly a lot windier. Anyway, last Tuesday (7th April 2015) I took both planes up to the flying site. It was a calm, sunny day and I counted at least 17 club members up there.

I didn't really want an audience, but both Alan (who had trained me to fly) and Les were there and they helped me check the plane. I firstly flew my IC trainer a couple of times, just to get my eye in. This was, after all, my first visit to the site since November and I still have difficulty lining up for landing and getting the plane where I want it on the strip. So I bent the front wheel leg again on my second landing.

Anyway, I couldn't put off the Cub's maiden flight any longer. Alan stood beside me to give advice and adjust trims. After an attempt at taxiing, where I found the rudder/tail wheel to be very sensitive, I took off. All went well. The Cub was quite stable and flew at a reasonable speed on only half throttle. I made several passes where I let go of the controls so that we could adjust the trims. After a while it was flying in a straight line, although it was affected by quite a strong breeze. Although I landed on the far side of the strip, I at least got it down gently and in one piece.

I forgot to set my timer (an app on my phone, which I set to 'flight' mode), but we guessed that I had been airborne for 6-7 minutes. When I checked the battery it was down to 16%! By this time it was getting late and I decided to quit while I was ahead, in spite of have another fully charged battery.

I will probably give it another flight or two before I tackle the decoration. It was quite tricky getting both planes and accessories into the car, so I might just take the Cub next time.

A Few More Flights

My next couple of flights were fine. However, whilst trying to taxi, the wheels stuck in the grass and the plane tipped forward causing the prop to hit the ground. I didn't notice any problem until I took off again. As the plane was passing I heard a strange noise, so I landed quickly. It seems that the prop hitting the ground had broken the motor mount and the plane was effectively being pulled through the air by the cowl! I had always been concerned about the slightness of the mount.

I will also need to ensure that I have some up-elevator when taxiing and starting to take off.
